Shortly after work you enter the supermarket, which is right around the corner. Because of its proximity, you almost always shop here. The shopping trolleys are right next to the entrance. You dig a coin out of your pocket and slide it into the coin compartment of the next trolley. With a single jerk, you pull the trolley out of the line-up and find the shopping list in your pocket. The writing on it is almost indecipherable. You steer the trolley through the various departments of the supermarket and keep an eye out for all the items you need according to the list. Apple, milk, eggs, sugar and flour all end up in your trolley one by one. Many other products try to tempt you, but you remain steadfast and go straight to the checkout. You pay for your purchases in cash and head home to bake an apple pie.
