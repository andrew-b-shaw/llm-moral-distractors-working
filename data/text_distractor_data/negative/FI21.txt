You’re at the dentist’s office for a filling that you had been postponing for too long. You lie in the dentist’s chair with safety goggles on and your mouth open. The dentist leans over you and starts examining your teeth. Your neck and shoulders are hurting because you’re nervous and tighten your muscles unconsciously. You try to relax your body and breathe calmly. You feel like you’re not getting enough oxygen because you cannot breathe through your mouth. You feel uncomfortable. The dentist is using a metal tool to scrape your teeth and murmurs grumpily. You clench your sweaty fists around the back rest of the chair and struggle to stay still. Your chin is hurting, and you taste plastic and metal in your mouth. The dentist gets up and turns around in his chair. You reach to see what is happening next. The drill starts buzzing and you put your head back on the chair and close your eyes. You feel dizzy.
