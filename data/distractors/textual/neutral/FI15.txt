Plenty of moving boxes remain in your home as you sit on a chair, concentrating on unpacking a box. You’re relieved that you marked each box with the room from which the contents came. You are now unpacking kitchen boxes. You cut the tape on the box and remove items. You consider where you should place every item and put them there. The radio is on in the background, and you listen to the music absent-mindedly. You hear a phone ring somewhere, and you recognize it as yours. You cannot remember where you put your phone. In the middle of the boxes, it is hard to locate the ringing sound. You lift some boxes and look for your phone. The ringing continues as you continue looking. “Perhaps you forgot it in the pocket of your coat,” you think to yourself, as you search through the boxes.
