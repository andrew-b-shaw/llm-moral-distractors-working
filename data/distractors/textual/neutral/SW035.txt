It's an ordinary Tuesday morning in the office. You're sitting on your office chair in front of your computer and are waiting until it boots. Then you hear a gentle ping. Like the sound of the proverbial pin falling to the ground. It's not a pin, it's a paper clip. Where did it come from? You don't have any paper clips anymore since you switched your office to paperless. You eyeball the utensil and you don't know why you're doing this. It's a simple paper clip. A small piece of bent metal wire with which you can hold sheets of paper together and later loosen them again. You wonder who invented it. How did the inventor come up with the idea? Did they just think of it that way? The inventor must have worked on it for a while. Maybe even years. Was it his life's work? If so, did he get any credit for it? Maybe you'll get to the root of it sometime. Someday. But not now: now, back to work.
