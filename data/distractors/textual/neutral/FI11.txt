It’s an ordinary weekly meeting at your workplace, and you’re all gathered in the meeting room. Your team leader is in charge of the meeting, and occasionally, others take part in the conversation. The meeting’s tempo is easygoing, and the atmosphere is very concentrated. You go through the agenda, which includes goals for the next few months and employees’ roles and tasks. This time, you don’t have an active role, and you can concentrate more on listening. On the table in front of you is a cup of hot coffee and your calendar, which you use for taking notes. You pick up a pen and follow the slide show projected on the wall while sipping your coffee. A colleague next to you leans toward you and whispers something. You need to struggle to hear her. You nod in understanding and answer in a low voice, covering your mouth with your hand. You then turn your attention back to the speaker and the ongoing presentation.
