On your birthday, someone gave you a bonsai olive tree. It was lovely! You have a little garden with a few plants, and you thought it would do just fine in a little corner which gets the sun both in summer and winter. And so you put it there. You had no idea how to look after bonsais, but you thought that if it grew, you would plant it in the same spot where you had put it. You really liked the idea of having a nice olive tree, and you liked the idea of the olives even more! However, that year, during the winter, you were studying for some really difficult public examinations. You had to pass them, and with decent marks, or you would end up on the street. All your efforts went into studying, and it didn't occur to you that it was too cold outside for the poor bonsai. When the examinations were finally over and winter gave way to some warmer days, you discovered, sadly, that the poor olive tree hadn't survived the frost. You felt really bad about that.
